Teaching

Courses I teach include:

  • ECO5064S: Behavioural Economics Behavioural economics is a relatively new field within economics. At its core is a strong interdisciplinary approach to modeling economic decision making. The course extends the framework of choice theory by incorporating two robust empirical findings about economic behaviour:
    • (i) People are boundedly rational.
    • (ii) People frequently behave in other-regarding ways.
    Topics include:
    • Risk attitudes with bounded rationality.
    • Risk preferences under stress and violence.
    • Rational altruism and altruistic punishment.
    • Trust and social cohesion.
    • Emotions and decision making.
  • ECO5000: Econometrics A course covering the fundamental techniques of econometrics, focused on real-world application and empirical data analysis.
  • ECO5076S: Development Microeconomics This course applies microeconomic analysis to issues of development in low- and middle-income countries. The main objectives include:
    • Evaluating the econometrics of impact evaluation.
    • Examining land contracts and land reform.
    • Intra-household resource allocation and gender bias.
    • The structure of labor and credit markets in developing countries.
