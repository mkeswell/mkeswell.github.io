Teaching

ECO5064S: Behavioural Economics
Behavioural economics is a relatively new field within economics. At its core is a strong interdisciplinary approach to modelling economic decision-making. This course extends the framework of choice theory by incorporating two robust empirical findings about economic behaviour: (i) people are boundedly rational and (ii) people frequently behave in other-regarding ways. The topics covered are: risk attitudes with bounded rationality, risk preferences under exposure to stress and violence, rational altruism and altruistic punishment, trust and social cohesion, and emotions and decision-making.

ECO5000: Econometrics
This course covers the fundamental techniques of econometrics, focusing on real-world application and empirical data analysis.

ECO5076S: Development Microeconomics
This course applies microeconomic analysis to issues of development in low- and middle-income countries. The main objectives include the econometrics of impact evaluation, land contracts and land reform, intra-household resource allocation and gender bias, the evaluation of human capital programs, the structure of labor and credit markets in developing countries, and the microeconomic impacts of international trade.
