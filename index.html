ABOUT ME

I am an Associate Professor in the school of Economics at the University of Cape Town. I have a Ph.D from the University of Massachusetts at Amherst (2003) and have held academic appointments at UCT (2001-2007, 2011-present) and Stellenbosch University (2007-2010). My research and teaching are in the fields of behavioural economics, experimental economics and development economics. I have served as a consultant to the World Bank, and have held visiting appointments at the Santa Fe Institute. Early in my career I held appointments at the University of Natal Medical School and the Parliament of the Republic of South Africa. I have won research grants from international organisations like the International Initiative for Impact Evaluation (3ie) as well as USAID. I was the 2005 recipient of the JJI Middleton Medal, awarded by the Economic Society of South Africa.

UNIVERSITY LEADERSHIP ROLES

I have served in several administrative roles most notably as Convenor of the Honours in Economics programme (2014-2016), as well as the Convenor of the Postgraduate Programmes in Economics (2020-2022) where I chaired the Economics Graduate Committee. I have also served on the following Faculty level committees between 2020 and 2022: the Higher Degrees Committee (HDC), the Doctoral Committee of Assessors (DCOA), and the Academic Policy and Planning Committee (APPC). Finally, I have also served on the Ethics in Research Committee (2018).
